Privacy Policy — NotesKeeper

Last updated: June 20, 2026

NotesKeeper is a note-taking app: you create notes containing text and photos (for example, to save the progress of a board game session). The content you create stays stored locally on your device and is not transmitted to our servers.

This policy describes how the NotesKeeper mobile application ("the app", "we") handles information about you. It applies to the Android app published under the identifier com.telen.noteskeeper.

1. Data controller

The data controller is Telen, the publisher of the app.

Contact: developer@telen.fr

2. Data we process

2.1. Content you create (stored only on your device)

When you use the app, you can create:

  • notes (title, date);
  • sub-notes (name, free text);
  • photos you take with your phone's camera.

This data is saved exclusively in the app's private storage on your device (local database and internal files). It is not transmitted to, accessed by, or stored by us or any third party. We have no access to it. You retain full control: you can delete it at any time from within the app, and uninstalling the app erases all of this content.

2.2. Device permissions

  • Camera (CAMERA): used only when you choose to take a photo to attach to a sub-note. The photo is saved in the app's private storage. The app does not access your gallery or your existing photos.
  • Internet access (INTERNET): used only to send the diagnostic reports described below. None of your notes, text, or photos are sent over this connection.

2.3. Diagnostic and stability data (Firebase Crashlytics)

To improve the reliability of the app, we use Firebase Crashlytics, a service provided by Google. When a crash or error occurs, Crashlytics automatically collects technical data, including:

  • a Crashlytics installation identifier (randomly generated);
  • crash reports and the associated stack traces;
  • information about the device and the app state at the time of the incident (device model, Android version, app version, available memory/storage, etc.).

This data does not contain the content of your notes or your photos. Collection is disabled in development builds and enabled in the published version, in order to detect and fix issues. The app does not use any advertising or audience-measurement analytics service.

4. Data retention

  • Content you create: kept on your device until you delete it or uninstall the app.
  • Diagnostic data: retained by Google in accordance with its retention periods. Crashlytics crash reports are generally kept for up to 90 days.

5. Recipients and processors

We do not sell or rent your data. The only third party that may process data on our behalf is Google Ireland Limited / Google LLC (Firebase Crashlytics), acting as a processor, for diagnostic purposes.

Processing by Google is governed by the Google Privacy Policy and the Firebase terms.

6. Transfers outside the European Union

Google/Firebase services may involve transferring data to countries outside the European Economic Area, in particular the United States. These transfers are covered by the appropriate safeguards required under the GDPR (European Commission Standard Contractual Clauses and Google's compliance mechanisms).

7. Security

The content you create is stored in the app's private storage, inaccessible to other apps under normal Android usage conditions. We recommend protecting your device (screen lock, encryption) to further safeguard the privacy of your local data.

8. Your rights

Under the GDPR, you have the rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, objection, and portability regarding your personal data.

  • Because the content you create is stored only on your device, you exercise these rights directly: view, edit, or delete your data within the app, or erase everything by uninstalling the app.
  • For the diagnostic data processed via Firebase, contact us at developer@telen.fr to exercise your rights. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (in France, the CNIL).

9. Children

NotesKeeper is a general-audience utility app. It is not directed to children under 15, and we do not knowingly collect data about them.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this privacy policy. Any change will be published at this same address, with an updated revision date. In the event of a significant change, we will make reasonable efforts to inform you by an appropriate means.
